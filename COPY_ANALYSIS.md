## What Could Be Sharper ⚠️

### 1. **The Intel Squad (MeetTheTeam) - Section Subtitle**

**CURRENT**:
```
"Meet the agentic team that breaks down the Bitcoin world for you.
No jargon, no hype—just honest explanations from people who actually
understand the space."
```

**ANALYSIS**:
- ✅ Accurate
- ⚠️ **Missing the Sofia Connection**: She doesn't yet feel like part of "your world" journey
- ⚠️ Repeats features already said above (no jargon, honest)
- ⚠️ No emotional hook for why she should care about these specific people

**RECOMMENDATION**: Make it more specific to Sofia's experience

**SUGGESTED REWRITE**:
```
"These aren't crypto experts talking at you. They're smart friends
explaining Bitcoin the way you'd explain it to Ana—specific, slightly
amused, never talking down."
```

**Why This Works**:
- References her real behavior (telling Ana)
- Creates intimacy ("smart friends")
- Shows personality ("slightly amused")
- Feels less corporate, more human

---

### 2. **Hero CTA Area**

**CURRENT**:
```
Button: "Start Reading"
Subtext: "Join 45,000+ subscribers."
```

**ANALYSIS**:
- ✅ Clear action
- ⚠️ "Start Reading" is passive (she's not reading yet, she's subscribing)
- ⚠️ "45,000+" might feel like herd mentality to Sofia
- ⚠️ The subtext doesn't connect to the promise above

**RECOMMENDATION**: Make the CTA match the promise

**SUGGESTED REWRITE**:
```
Button: "Get Today's Issue"
Subtext: "Free. No spam. Ever."
```

**Why This Works**:
- Action-oriented ("Get")
- Specific ("Today's Issue" = she's getting real content)
- Removes pressure ("No spam")
- "Ever" echoes the About page trust message

## Copy Voice Guidelines (For Your Website Going Forward)

Keep the existing rules from your DNA doc, plus add:

1. **Always Answer "So What?"**
   - Not: "Goldman Sachs announced support for Bitcoin"
   - Yes: "Goldman Sachs decided Bitcoin was safe enough for them. It only took about fifteen years."

2. **Include Sofia's Perspective**
   - Every major section should hint at why Sofia should care
   - Use her currency (€), her city (sometimes), her reality (phone, lunch break)

3. **Trust > Hype**
   - Counter every positive claim with a real-world acknowledgment
   - "No pressure. Ever." works because it's honest

4. **One Intrigue Take Per Page**
   - Just like your articles, your website should have a perspective
   - Right now it's all explanation. Add at least one opinionated moment per page

5. **Mobile-First Copy**
   - Every sentence should work on a phone screen
   - This website is now optimized for mobile. Use shorter sentences here too.
